About Me

I am a Project Manager/GIS Analyst and partner at Line 45, a small company based in Gaylord, Michigan offering application development, GIS services, aerial imagery & remote sensing, and software consulting. We specialize in developing solutions for companies, non-profits, and government agencies in the environmental and natural resource sectors. I’m also currently pursuing a master’s degree in GIS and Web Map Programming at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

I first began using maps for navigation on family canoe trips to the Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness in northern Minnesota. I continued exploring the backcountry and honing my map reading skills at Earlham College where I was involved with the Wilderness Program - first as a student and then as an instructor. Needless to say, when I began using GIS on the job I was hooked. I enjoy bringing the power of GIS analysis and the beauty of great visualization to client projects.

Throughout my career I’ve held positions requiring a variety of skill sets including project management, technical writing, and spatial data analysis. I’m comfortable with both open source (QGIS) and Esri (ArcMap) GIS software. More recently I’ve been working with Python, SQL, HTML/CSS/JavaScript, and LeafletJS.

Feel free to get in touch. I'd love to hear from you!
